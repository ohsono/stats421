# Housing Market Investment Analysis for 2026
## California to Texas Migration & Emerging Market Opportunities

**Analysis Date:** November 29, 2025  
**Investment Timeframe:** 2026 and Beyond  
**Focus:** CA→TX Migration, ROI Analysis, Salary Data, Market Predictions

---

## Executive Summary

The housing market presents compelling investment opportunities for 2026, particularly in Texas markets benefiting from California's continued out-migration (102,442 people in 2022, holding steady at ~100K annually). Key findings:

- **Texas leads nation** in net domestic migration: +85,267 (2024)
- **California lost** 239,575 residents (2024) - largest outmigration of any state
- **Texas rental yields:** 7-10% across major metros vs. national averages of 4-6%
- **Median home prices:** TX $342,167 vs. CA $746,667 (2024)
- **Projected mortgage rates:** 5.6-6.0% by end of 2025
- **Home price growth:** Expected to slow to 3.8% (2025) and 3.6% (2026)

## 2. California to Texas Migration Analysis

### Migration Volume & Demographics

**2024 Migration Data:**
- **California → Texas:** ~105,000 people annually (consistent since 2022)
- **Texas net gain from CA:** 54,136 households bringing avg $145,960 AGI
- **Reverse flow (TX → CA):** 23,316 households
- **Net household gain:** Approximately 30,820 households annually

**Top Destination Routes:**
1. **Los Angeles → Austin** (primary tech corridor)
2. **San Jose → Austin** (tech talent migration)
3. **Los Angeles → Fort Worth** (affordability seekers)
4. **Santa Clara → Fort Worth** (tech + lower costs)
5. **San Francisco → Austin** (lifestyle + career)

**Income Profile of Migrants:**
- **From California:** Avg household AGI $145,960
- **From New York:** Avg household AGI $109,467
- **Wealthiest leaving TX for:** Massachusetts ($175K+ AGI)

### Migration Drivers

**Push Factors (California):**
- **Housing Costs:** Median home price $909,400 (2025 projected)
- **Median rent:** $2,572 for 2BR vs. $1,565 in Texas
- **State income tax:** 1-13.3% vs. 0% in Texas
- **Cost of living:** 30.6% higher wages needed vs. Texas just for basics
- **Wildfires:** 1 in 10 buildings at high risk
- **Tax burden:** Sales tax 7.25-10.75%

**Pull Factors (Texas):**
- **Housing affordability:** Median $342,167 vs. CA $746,667
- **No state income tax:** Major draw for high earners
- **Job market:** +284,000 jobs (Dec 2023-Dec 2024) = 2.0% growth
- **Corporate relocations:** Chevron, HP, Oracle, Tesla (Austin)
- **Business-friendly:** Fast LLC setup, landlord-friendly laws
- **Lower overall costs:** Despite higher property taxes (1.6-2.2%)

## 6. Market Risks & Considerations

### Texas-Specific Risks

**Property Taxes:**
- **Rate:** 1.6-2.2% of assessed value (HIGH)
- **Mitigation:** Offset by no state income tax
- **Agricultural exemption:** Available for qualifying land (1-d-1)

**Market Saturation:**
- **Multifamily:** Over-delivered in some markets (Austin)
- **Apartment deliveries 2025:** ~55,000 units (half of 2024)
- **Rent growth:** Flat to slightly negative in overbuilt areas
- **Single-family:** Continued shortage in most metros

**Climate & Insurance:**
- **Extreme weather:** Hurricanes (coastal), storms
- **Insurance costs:** Rising (factor into underwriting)
- **Utility costs:** Higher AC costs in summer (~$186/month avg)

**Regulatory:**
- **Short-term rentals:** City-specific regulations (check HOAs)
- **Hotel occupancy tax:** Applies to STRs
- **Zoning:** Varies by municipality

### National Market Risks

**Interest Rate Sensitivity:**
- **Current levels:** ~6.75-7.0% still constraining buyers
- **Fed policy uncertainty:** Tariffs and immigration policy impact
- **Inflation risk:** Could delay rate cuts

**Inventory Dynamics:**
- **Rate lock effect:** 82% of mortgaged homeowners <6% (Q4 2024)
- **Slow inventory release:** Unless rates drop 1%+ quickly
- **Supply constraints:** Decade of under-construction

**Economic Uncertainty:**
- **Recession risk:** Low but present
- **Tariff impacts:** Construction costs could rise
- **Immigration policy:** Labor supply for construction
